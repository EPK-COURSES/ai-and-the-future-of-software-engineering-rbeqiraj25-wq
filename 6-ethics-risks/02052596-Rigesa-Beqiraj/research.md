-Introduction
Today, Artificial Intelligence is transforming the way software is developed. Programmers usually use AI for code generation, automated testing, debugging and documentation. So it has become the center of technology. Of course, tools like ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot and Google Gemini come to the rescue to write code as quickly as possible and to automate various tasks. As advantageous as AI is, on the other hand there are many disadvantages where its uncontrolled use brings ethical risks, security and technical limitations. Here the question arises: Is AI really trustworthy? Is the privacy and security of systems at risk? Based on these questions, you understand that, although AI can be useful, it can also become scary when it is not under control.
 This research paper analyzes the main risks of AI including hallucinations,incorrect code genration, security vulnerabilities, privacy concerns, overdependence on AI , copyright issues, and ethical responsibility.
 
 -Hallucinations and Incorrect Code Generation
The main problem with AI is the phenomenon known as “hallucination”. This happens when AI produces answers that seem correct, but are actually wrong. Because considering that programmers are increasingly relying on AI, this can affect when they will accept a complete solution from AI and use it without testing it properly. What makes the situation worse is the illusion that the solution is correct. This makes it even more difficult for beginners and cannot identify errors. According to researchers at Stanford University, AI models can suggest unsafe and inefficient solutions even when the user is looking for safe programming practices. This happens because AI does not understand the logic of the problem like a human, it only predicts texts based on data  exist. So they cause: application failures, incorrect syntax, security problems and inefficient algorithms. Therefore AI is not a good replacement for a programmer.

-Security Vulnerabilities and Cyber ​​Attacks
Another equally important problem is the generation of code with security weaknesses. So it can indeed produce code that works but contains security vulnerabilities.
 Some concrete examples are:
Insecure password management
Weak server configurations
Weak authentication
SQL Injection
Cross-Site Scripting
When developers blindly trust AI without checking the security of the code, applications are expected to be attacked by hackers at any moment. Something even more serious is that AI can be used by anyone, including attackers. They use it to automate attacks, analyze vulnerabilities, create malware, and generate attack scripts. Security experts say that developers should combine AI with manual control, code review, and penetration testing. If organizations focus only on development speed and not on security, the risk of cyber incidents will increase significantly. This is where we really understand how dangerous AI is.

-Privacy and Data Protection Concerns
Another  serious concern is privacy and data protection. Because programmers copy private code into AI chatbots when needed, there is a risk that this data will be stored by the system and the company may lose confidential information.
A concrete example is a case where some Samsung employees used ChatGPT to analyze internal company code. As a result, problems arose over the distribution of confidential information and the company imposed restrictions on the use of AI.
The main privacy issues are:
Loss of company secrets 
Legal issues
Unauthorized use of data
Lack of transparency
Exposure of personal data
In the European Union, regulations such as the GDPR require strong protection of personal data. However, many AI systems are not fully transparent about how information is stored and used. 
For this reason, organizations must create clear security policies and professional ethics.

- Overdependence on AI and Skill degradation
Another huge risk is the over-reliance on AI. Nowadays, many programmers are relying on AI for even the most basic programming tasks such as writing code, debugging, documentation, and optimization.
Although this may seem like a positive thing in the long run, it actually causes developers to lose their algorithmic logic, critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and debugging. Here we cannot deny that those who are worst affected are students and beginners. Because, they will not get a strong foundation from the beginning if the learning process is replaced by automation. And if you think about it, the industry could face a shortage of experts who deeply understand the systems.

-Copyright and Intellectual Property Issues
Copyright and Intellectual Property Issues are another topic that is both serious and interesting. Seeing the rapid and advanced development of AI that includes copyrighted code, articles and materials, I often find myself thinking 
Are copyrighted materials being used without permission?
Is the code really original?
Who owns the code generated by AI?
But regarding this topic there is a lot of legal uncertainty, so many court processes are underway to establish clearer and fairer rules for content creators and users of AI technologies. In the future, we will need new legal rules and ethical standards for the use of AI.

-Ethical Responsibility of Engineers
Another topic as interesting as the previous one is the ethical responsibility of engineers. When AI causes a problem, who is responsible?
This question, which is very important, especially in the world we live in today, is a question that arouses everyone's curiosity.
But if an AI system produces code that causes security problems, data exposure, and financial losses, who is responsible in this case?
Those who must account for this are those who created the "engineers". That is why in the future, the role of the engineer will also be the critical oversight of intelligent technologies.

-Conclusion
We come to the conclusion that AI, although it has many advantages,it also has many strong disadvantages. AI is advancing day by day and is transforming the software industry. We learned what the risks of AI were such as: hallucinations, security vulnerabilities, privacy problems, overdependence on AI and copyright issues. Risks that cannot be ignored. 
In my opinion, after this research I was even more convinced that AI is irreplaceable. Also, human intelligence, critical thinking and professional responsibility are the true traits that a person who calls himself an engineer must possess.
